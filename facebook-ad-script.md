## SHOT-BY-SHOT FILMING GUIDE

| Timestamp | What to Film | Camera Direction |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| 0–3s | You, direct to camera — deliver the hook | Good lighting, clean background. High energy, almost urgent. |
| 3–12s | You, conversational | Slight movement (walking, gesturing). Relatable frustration — like you've been there. Shake your head slightly. |
| 12–22s | You + device/screen | Hold up your phone showing SWFT or gesture to a monitor. Energy shifts UP — this is the solution moment. |
| 22–38s | B-roll screen recordings with voiceover | Pre-record screen captures of each SWFT feature. You narrate over them. Quick cuts, 1–2 seconds each. |
| 38–48s | You, slower and personal + price comparison graphic | Build trust. Eye contact. Speak like you're talking to a friend. The comparison graphic appears beside you or as a full-screen overlay. |
| 48–57s | You, strong close | Confident. Clear. Point down at the end (toward the CTA link in the ad). Slight smile — you know this is a no-brainer. |

## PRE-FILMING CHECKLIST

- [ ] Script printed or loaded into teleprompter app
- [ ] All 10 screen recordings of SWFT features captured and saved
- [ ] Clean, professional background (solid wall, office, or slightly blurred)
- [ ] Good lighting — ring light or natural window light facing you (not behind you)
- [ ] Phone or camera at eye level on a tripod or stable surface
- [ ] Audio: Use a lapel mic ($15–20 on Amazon) or AirPods — avoid built-in phone mics (they pick up echo)
- [ ] Wear a solid-color shirt (no busy patterns — they distract on camera)
- [ ] Film multiple takes of the hook (first 3 seconds) — at least 5 versions
- [ ] Have water nearby — dry mouth shows on camera

---

## POST-PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

1. **Import footage** into CapCut or Descript
2. **Cut to script** — remove pauses, "ums," filler words, and bad takes
3. **Insert screen recording B-roll** over the feature callout section (seconds 22–38)
4. **Add text overlays** — feature names in bold, readable font (white text, dark background or drop shadow)
5. **Build the price comparison graphic** — animate the "Without SWFT vs. With SWFT" breakdown (use Canva to design the graphic, import into CapCut)
6. **Add auto-captions** — use CapCut or Submagic for bold, animated style
7. **Add background music** — low volume, upbeat but not distracting (pull from Facebook Sound Collection for zero licensing risk)
8. **Add end card** — SWFT logo, goswft.com URL, "14-Day Free Trial" text
9. **Export in two formats:**
   - 1:1 (1080x1080) for Facebook/Instagram Feed
   - 9:16 (1080x1920) for Reels/Stories
10. **Design thumbnail** in Canva — use a frame of you + bold text overlay: "Stop Losing Jobs" or "14 Days Free"
11. **Upload to Facebook Ads Manager** and configure campaign per the settings above

## KEY REMINDERS

- **First 3 seconds decide everything.** If they don't stop scrolling, nothing else matters. Film the hook 5 different ways and pick the best one.
- **Captions are mandatory.** 85% of Facebook video is watched on mute. If they can't read it, they scroll past.
- **One CTA only.** Don't confuse people. The single action is: start the 14-day free trial.
- **Feature stacking = perceived value.** The rapid-fire feature section (9 features in 16 seconds) makes viewers think "all of THAT for one price?"
- **The value comparison is your strongest weapon.** $464–$759/mo separately vs. $279/mo with SWFT — that's directly from your website and it's a devastating comparison.
- **The 14-day free trial removes all friction.** Mentioned twice in the script (middle and close). "No charge today. Cancel anytime." — exact wording from goswft.com.
- **Speak to home service pros specifically.** Don't say "small business." Say "contractors, plumbers, HVAC techs" — they need to hear their own job title to pay attention.
